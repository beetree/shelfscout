What this report tells a brand-side reader

  • Which independent retailers in DFW actually carry the bottle on shelf
  • How those stores are pricing it — and whether anyone is significantly undercutting suggested retail

  • Which stores will hold a bottle for an inbound customer (a real proxy for shelf engagement)

Stores requiring follow-up

8 stores rang or routed through chain phone systems without producing actionable data on this cycle (mostly DTMF-required IVRs at major local chains). Standard practice is to retry these on the next reporting cycle with adjusted call routing.

Pricing observation

  • Spread of $20.00 between the lowest ($59.99) and highest ($79.99) observed shelf prices for the same SKU within one metro
  • Three of six priced stores cluster near $59.99–$71.99 (mid-market positioning)
  • Two stores hold at $79.99 (premium-positioned independents)
  • This level of pricing variance is normal for indie-channel; the value of the data is identifying outliers and tracking direction over time

Hold-willingness observation

  • 8/8 confirmed in-stock stores agreed to hold a bottle for an inbound customer
  • 100% hold rate on a sought-after allocated bourbon is a meaningful signal of shelf engagement: stores treat the SKU as a relationship product, not just inventory
